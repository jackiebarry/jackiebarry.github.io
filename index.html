About Me

Hi everyone! My name is Jackie Barry and I am an intermediate/secondary science teacher in St. John’s, NL!

Education

I grew up in Ottawa before moving to St. John's to pursue my degree in Biochemistry (Nutrition) with a minor in Biology. I then completed a Masters of Science in Medicine in Immunology and Infectious Diseases with a focus in Virology. Following my MSc, I completed my BEd (Int/Sec) and have started my Bachelor of Special Education (September 2019).

Work

I thrive in a team environment, and enjoy sharing my passion for learning. I consider myself to be a “lifetime student,” and am eager to build my knowledge base of innovative technologies and teaching strategies.

I am currently preparing for the upcoming school year, and am looking forward to experiencing different schools as a substitute teacher! By the way, check out my view of teaching..

Teaching Philosophy

Passion for Learning

I always believed that I would be working in some capacity that allows me to make a positive impact on kids' lives. When reflecting on those who have helped to shape me as an individual, I often think of a few select teachers I had throughout my life as a student. These individuals represent the type of teacher I aspire to be; they were all kind, considerate, and involved and pushed all of their students to do the best they can. I hope to have a positive influence, both in and out of the classroom, on all my future students.

Open Door Policy

I want to be the teacher that students feel comfortable speaking with and coming to for help. I believe that all students need to have a positive role model; one who is approachable, respectful, and patient. I want all of my students to know that I respect them as individuals, and that I value their opinions and beliefs. I aspire to be compassionate and understanding, especially when it comes to designing lesson plans that are accommodating and inclusive for all students in my classroom. I believe that, although it may be more work, differentiating instruction to accommodate diverse learners is beneficial for all students in the classroom.

Impact of my teachers

My teaching philosophy is based on the values I appreciated in my teachers. I try everyday to be compassionate, kind and welcoming. I want to provide a classroom environment that students feel is a safe place for them, where they can ask questions and grow as individuals. I also intend to make sure all of the needs of my students are met. I hope to help all of my students succeed in all facets of their lives.

Extracurriculars

Past Involvement

As a student, I was super involved in a number of different teams and clubs at my school. I loved participating because you develop a sense of community in your school and camaraderie with your peers. I hope to encourage my future students to engage in activities and get involved!

Interests and Hobbies

I still have a wide range of interests and hobbies which I believe can translate well into extracurricular activities at schools! As an athlete, I have played soccer and rugby, learned to wrestle (through my coaching endeavours), and run with cross-country and track and field. As an avid outdoors woman, I love to camp, fish, hike and hunt. In addition, I also love to read, knit play cards and board games.

Future Involvement

As a teacher, I plan to be as involved in extracurriculars as I was when I was a student. This past year when working at the Sheshatshiu Innu School, I became involved with the community’s wrestling team as a coach. It was one of the most rewarding experiences I have had. I can’t wait to get involved with (or establish) clubs or sports wherever I end up as a teacher!

News

This fall marks the beginning of a brand new academic adventure for me!! In addition to being a substitute teacher, I’m starting the Special Education program at Memorial University! I am really looking forward to learning all the tips and tricks of supporting diverse learners and can’t wait to get started!

Cheers to any other lifetime students out there who can’t seem to stay away from additional educational experiences! Thanks for checking in on what’s new with me, I’ll keep you all posted as the adventure continues!
